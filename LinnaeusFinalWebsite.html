How To Predict the Future using Math: A Tutorial

How? We need to understand Logarithms

A logarithm is a mathematical operation that explains the exponent to which a particular number should be raised to generate another number. In simpler terms, it is a way of expressing the relationship between numbers that are multiplied or divided. Logarithms are also mathematical functions that are the inverse or opposite of exponentials. The process of taking logs is the undoing of exponentials, as it allows us to find the original value before it was raised to a particular power. The notation for a logarithm comprises the base number as a subscript after the word "log."

The two most commonly used types of logarithms are base 10 logarithms - known as common logarithms and natural logarithms - which have their unique notation.

Oh... we also need to know what Natural Logarithms are



The natural logarithm, also known as the base-e log, is a valuable concept in mathematics. The value of e, which represents the natural exponential, is approximately equal to 2.71828 . This logarithm has a unique notation, ln(x), and is commonly pronounced as "el-en-of-x". It was first introduced by the famous mathematician Leonhard Euler in the 18th century.


Understanding the natural logarithm and its properties can be useful in various mathematical applications.
